Future survey campaigns should front-load either personalization or the brand name, and test urgency framing ('Last call,' 'Last chance') earlier in the campaign sequence rather than reserving it for reminders.

    What this tells us

    Top-performing webinar emails in this dataset fall into two distinct clusters: small-list transactional messages (post-event thank-yous, on-demand confirmations, and physical gift incentives) sent to fewer than 100 recipients, and urgency-driven 'last chance' re-engagement emails to mid-sized lists (250–1,500). The high open rates on tiny lists likely reflect a warm, self-selected audience rather than subject line efficacy, so the real actionable signal comes from the mid-list performers: urgency framing, tangible incentives like coffee kits or gift boxes, and on-demand availability hooks consistently outperform generic invitation or countdown copy at comparable list sizes. Across the full dataset, click-through rate is the more reliable engagement signal for large sends—open rates above 25% on lists over 5,000 are achievable but clicks rarely exceed 2%, suggesting subject lines are getting opens but body content or CTAs are not converting. The clearest opportunity is to combine urgency language with a specific physical or content incentive, a formula that appears in several of the strongest mid-list performers.

    Strong Signal

    'Last chance' and urgency subject lines reliably outperform standard invitation subject lines on mid-sized lists (500–3,500 recipients).

    'Last chance: Secure your spot for Demo Day' achieved 48.8% open rate (n=1,021) vs. 26.3% for the initial invitation to the same event (n=3,543)

    Try this next: Always plan a dedicated 'last chance' send 1–3 days before registration closes; use explicit 'Last Chance' or 'Last chance:' phrasing in the subject line rather than countdown language like '1 week away.'

    On-demand availability framing ('available on demand', 'Watch on demand') drives strong open and click rates, especially for post-event follow-up.

    'Demo Day available on demand' achieved 66.7% open rate and 26.7% click rate (n=15); 'Watch Medallion Demo Day on demand' achieved 44.0% open and 20.0% click (n=25)

    Try this next: Send a dedicated on-demand follow-up email within 48 hours of every webinar or event, leading with 'available on demand' or 'Watch on demand' in the subject line, and target both registrants who did not attend and the broader interested audience.

    Personalization tokens used as subject line openers (firstname merge tags) do not produce above-average open rates on large lists.

    Both firstname-token emails achieved 22.8% and 20.0% open rates on lists of 10,864 and 10,332—below the dataset median for all sends

    Try this next: Audit personalization token implementation before sending; if firstname data is incomplete, use a non-personalized urgency subject line, which this data shows outperforms broken or generic token fallbacks.

    Large-list sends (10,000+) have a hard ceiling around 25% open rate and rarely exceed 1% click rate, regardless of subject line approach.

    Six sends over 10,000 recipients averaged 21.8% open rate and 0.6% click rate; no send over 10,000 exceeded 25.7% open or 1.0% click

    Try this next: Segment large broadcast lists by engagement tier or persona before sending; reserve full-list blasts for high-priority announcements only, and test segmented sends with tailored subject lines against the control broadcast to measure uplift.
